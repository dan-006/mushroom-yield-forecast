Linear Regression Coefficient Interpretation



The temperature-humidity interaction feature received the largest positive coefficient, indicating that the combined effect of temperature and humidity is strongly associated with mushroom yield. This suggests that favorable temperature conditions are most beneficial when accompanied by appropriate humidity levels.



CO₂ concentration showed a small positive coefficient, indicating a modest positive relationship with yield when other variables are held constant.



Temperature and humidity individually received negative coefficients. Because the temperature-humidity interaction feature is highly correlated with temperature, these coefficients are affected by multicollinearity and should not be interpreted as direct biological effects. The overall model performance is more reliable than the individual coefficient signs.



From an agritech perspective, the results suggest that environmental variables act together rather than independently. Maintaining a balanced growing environment may be more important than optimizing any single sensor measurement.
